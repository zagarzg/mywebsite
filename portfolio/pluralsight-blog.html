#FREEApril on Pluralsight...

As the situation with COVID-19 in the world got worse. Pluralsight decided to make their online courses completely free during the month of April. It was a great chance for us developers to learn new things during the quarantine.

I knew Pluralsight is a great source of knowledge for alot of developers but since I am a student, it felt preety expensive for me, so when I saw the platform is free I was very excited. My goal for April was to learn as much as I can on the platform and at the end of the month evaluate my skillIQ on the site.

If you can afford Pluralsight, I would definetly recommend it. I gives you great learning structure which you can follow and you don't worry what to learn next.

I started with the JavaScript path, since I felt like I could supplement my freeCodeCamp lessons with it. I must say JavaScript path is structured really nicely and the tutors are really eloquent at explaining concepts. It took me about 20hours to complete it so I was really binge-watching the first week.

Next I enrolled in HTML and CSS paths and I finished them by the end of the month. I also did skillIQ evaluation in all three paths. I was very pleased with how much I know, I scored 256 in HTML, 164 in CSS and 220 in JavaScript. In future I am thinking about enrolling in React/Redux course.
